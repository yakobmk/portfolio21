Tour and Travel

A responsive, tour and travel web application that allows users to experience places around Ethiopia through booking tickets to different upcoming trips; to better curate a smooth experience and that addresses doubts that come from uncertainty, we also incorporated live scenes from past trips. In addition, we also added interaction with tour guides and local partners prior to trips to those places to foster prior connection and or create new opportunities and possibilities.

The challenge

The aim of this project was aimed at creating a seamless exploration of tours and travel around Ethiopia. This led to ask question like: How can we create an environment that reduces uncertainty and doubt for fellow travelers and explorers? How can we provide more information about key details of trips without cluttering or redundancy? What are key components to create a smooth searching and filtering process?

Landing Page

Putting our fellow travelers and our local destination partners in conjunction with the perceived notion of how complicated things get when it comes to booking a ticket or traveling somewhere, we realized that simplicity is our greatest friend. In pursuing this we took inspiration from Netflix. To create familiarization with the product as well as the site, we took inspiration from Netflix by placing our featured trip at the top of the home page.

Options & Journey

Upon deciding options and on journey display method we carried out different iterations which we then took towards potential users and iterated over them. In the end we found out that instead of having a homepage that included a little bit of everything (the latest trips, partners, and an about us section) our design that combined the discover page as the home page created a smooth user experience for users.

Users start at our landing page where they are presented with an organized list of contents. Vertically differentiated by trip types and horizontally scrollable contents of trips within that specific activity type.

In the journeys of our tasked users we also found the addition of a bottom horizontal nav bar served users with great functionality as usersâ€™ major concern included wanting to travel to a specific destination and or traveling through a specific amount of time. For this reason we found our destination and search button to be the most clicked.

Way Finding

One of the main functionalities that fellow travelers could depend on is our search page, hence, upon brainstorming on what key features about a particular trip that an explorer might search for, we narrowed down our list to be destination, date, length of travel and activity type.

On the other hand, in the cases where a user might not particularly what kind of a trip he/she would want to be a part of while wanting to go towards a particular destination, we decided to have a destinations page where users could browse through the list of places that our tours will be exploring in conjunction with a similar filtering process included in our home page.

Onward Journey

To keep clutter to the minimum, we kept text minimal until clicked; and upon a click, users are taken to a product page that includes details of the trip with the ability to book the trip, add it to their wish list, and or share the trip information with loved ones.
Upon clicking the home icon from the bottom nav bar or either a drag down from the project page users are taken back to the home page or to the page in which they were at before they clicked on the trip.

In addition, users are also presented with trip suggestions based on either the destination or the trip activity users had selected upon further scrolling down on the selected trip page.
